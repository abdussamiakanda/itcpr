From Intern to PhD: ITCPR's Photonics Researcher Joins Oklahoma's Doctoral Program

July 18, 2025
By ITCPR Team

We are excited to share a proud moment for the ITCPR community. Tonmoy Malakar, a dedicated researcher with ITCPR's Photonics Group and a graduate of our internship program, has been offered admission to the PhD program at The University of Oklahoma, United States.

Tonmoy joined ITCPR as an intern in 2024, quickly standing out for his sharp research skills, enthusiasm, and collaborative spirit. His internship laid the groundwork for a deeper engagement with the Photonics Research Group, where he was promoted to a member ever since.

The University of Oklahoma - known for its cutting-edge research in photonics and materials science - recognized Tonmoy's potential and offered him a place in their doctoral program. This marks a significant milestone in his academic journey and is also a proud reflection of the mentorship and research environment we foster at ITCPR.

At ITCPR, Tonmoy has been involved in various research projects, including designing advanced photonic sensors and exploring photonic crystals for optical applications in sensors. His work has not only advanced our research goals but also inspired fellow researchers within our community.

Tonmoy's contributions - particularly in photonic simulations, and photonic crystal design - have earned recognition through publications and collaborative projects under the ITCPR banner. His work reflects both his personal dedication and the collaborative ethos we nurture across our research groups.

His profile is outlined below:

  • Academic Background:

  • Publications: 1

Receiving this PhD offer is a testament to Tonmoy's perseverance, academic excellence, and the research foundation built during his time with ITCPR. We are proud to see our members achieving such milestones and setting examples for the next generation of young researchers.

“At ITCPR, we are committed to nurturing talent and giving young researchers the platform they need to grow and excel,” said Md Abu Jafar Pikul, Executive Director of ITCPR. “We are thrilled to see Tonmoy taking this next step in his academic career and look forward to his continued impact-both at ITCPR and beyond.”

Even as he embarks on his PhD journey, Tonmoy will remain a valued part of the ITCPR research network, continuing to collaborate on projects and contribute to the community.

We extend our heartfelt congratulations to him on this remarkable achievement and look forward to witnessing his continued growth as a researcher and member of the global scientific community.
